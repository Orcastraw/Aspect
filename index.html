This is Aspect Tool, which will help you stream games at the proper and highest quality for its pixel ratios.

PC games are flexible and typically can scale to whatever resolution you give them. 16:9 is the most common and preferable, but some games support 16:9 & 4:3. Here's an example of both.

All Xbox 360 and Xbox One games are in 16:9, and should look like this

All native WiiU games are 16:9, and should look like this.

All PS3 and PS4 games are in 16:9, and should look like this.

Both DS screens are in 4:3, and should look like this.

The 3DS' 2 screens are differing ratios, with the top being a wider, 5:3 aspect ratio and the bottom being in 4:3.

GBA games are in the 3:2 aspect ratio, and should look like this.

Gameboy and Gameboy Color games have a native resolution of 160x144, which loosely translates to 10:9, and should look like this.

Gamecube games are special in that they have largely UNIQUE aspect ratios.

Most Wii games support 4:3 AND 16:9. However some only support one or the other. To select an aspect ratio, simply select Widescreen or Standard in Wii System Settings for 16:9 and 4:3 respectively.

PSP games are in 30:17, which is 2 pixels short of 16:9. The difference is minor, and you should just treat it like 16:9. PS Vita games are all 16:9 as well. Your games should look like this.

Most retro games are 4:3, and should look like this. Once you get your game set up, you can stream it looking like this.
