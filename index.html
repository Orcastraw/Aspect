This is Aspect Tool, which will help you stream games at the proper and highest quality for its pixel ratios.

PC games are flexible and typically can scale to whatever resolution you give them. 16:9 is the most common and preferable, but some games support 16:9 & 4:3. Here's an example of both.

All Xbox 360 and Xbox One games are in 16:9, and should look like the image on the right. The Xbox 360 also supports 4:3 for both Xbox 360 and backwards compatability Xbox games.

All native WiiU games are 16:9, and should look like the image on the right. However for Virtual Console games, you should ignore the black bars and refer to the respective games column's for how to crop them.

All PS3 and PS4 games are in 16:9, and should look like this. However for PS1 games on PS3, you should refer to the Retro Games' column.

Both DS screens are in 4:3, and should look like this. Generally it's reccomended for DS games that when streamed, you have the top screen bigger than the bottom and have the bottom on the side.

The 3DS' 2 screens are differing ratios, with the top being a wider, 5:3 aspect ratio and the bottom being in 4:3. Generally it's reccomended for DS games that when streamed, you have the top screen bigger than the bottom and have the bottom on the side.

GBA games are in the 3:2 aspect ratio, and should look like this. When streaming or capturing the game it's fine to show it with either the original aspect ratio or a 4:3 border if a Gameboy Player is being used.

Gameboy and Gameboy Color games have a native resolution of 160x144, which loosely translates to 10:9, and should look like this. When streaming or capturing the game it's fine to show it with either the original aspect ratio or a 4:3 border if a Gameboy Player is being used.

Gamecube games are special in that they have largely UNIQUE aspect ratios. Some GameCube games also support 16:9, which is easy and nice, and sadly, very rare.

Most Wii games support 4:3 AND 16:9. However some only support one or the other. To select an aspect ratio, simply select Widescreen or Standard in Wii System Settings for 16:9 and 4:3 respectively.

PSP games are in 30:17, which is 2 pixels short of 16:9. The difference is minor, and you should just treat it like 16:9. PS Vita games are all 16:9 as well. Your games should look like this. For streaming PS1 games off of a PSP, refer to the Retro Games' column.

Most retro games are 4:3, and should look like this. Once you get your game set up, you can stream it looking like this.
